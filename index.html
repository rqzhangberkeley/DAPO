Introduction

Reinforcement-learning (RL) fine-tuning with verifiable rewards has become the de-facto route to strong mathematical reasoning LLMs. Unfortunately, uniform prompt sampling spends many of its compute on trivial or impossible questions—wasting GPU time and yielding noisy gradients.

SPEED-RL tackles this inefficiency with a fully online curriculum learning method: we first generate a handful of rollouts per prompt to estimate its pass-rate, discard those that look too easy or too hard, and only then commit to the expensive full inference and gradient update.

Theoretically, we prove prompts with pass-rates near 0 or 1 have extremely low signal-to-noise ratio of standard policy-gradient estimators. Empirically, our implementation accelerates RL training 2×–6× on Qwen2.5-Math models across five public benchmarks on math reasoning.
